Behavioural Taster Test

If ever you have wondered why you find it easier to get on with some people, and not others or why members of your team respond better to you it's probably because your styles and approaches are aligned.

What do we mean? Take our Behavioural Taster Test to find out a little more.

In the questions that follow you will be presented with four options. Click one green thumbs up for the option that's most like you, and click one red thumb down, for an option that's least like you.

Overall, I would describe myself as:

  • Thoughtful and industrious
  • Willing and supportive
  • Pragmatic and forceful
  • Stimulating and creative

Where there's interference on a project, I usually think it's best to:

  • Stick to a logical, systematic, proven approach
  • Find out how others are feeling and make sure we can agree on a procedure
  • Concentrate on getting what I want accomplished right now
  • Be original and say what I think

I like it when others tell me they think I'm:

  • Intellectually gifted
  • A dependable person who comes through for them
  • A person who knows where he or she is going and is going to get there
  • Creative and stimulating

In difficult situations, my approach sometimes results in being:

  • Too involved with concepts and ideas alone
  • So concerned with others that I don't think about my own situation
  • Too concerned with here and now and getting and doing what 1 want
  • So concerned with settling the battle and wanting to get to the future that I might misjudge the present situation

When others pressure me, I am inclined to be overly:

  • Analytic and critical of others
  • Concerned with what others think and tend not to take action
  • Concerned with proving my self with immediate actions
  • Emotional and get carried away with my feelings

I find I am most convincing when I am able to:

  • Use logic and facts to persuade people to my point of view
  • Be in touch with my own feelings and resonate with those of others
  • Present options to people and help them choose an alternative
  • Stimulate people with new ideas and excite them into action

I feel satisfied with myself when I:

  • Solve a problem using a logical method
  • Understand and respond in a helpful way - to the feelings of others
  • Get more things accomplished than I've planned
  • Develop new thoughts and create ideas that can be implemented

Sometimes, when my behaviour seems extreme, others might feel I am:

  • Highly unemotional or detached
  • Dependent, conforming or unsure
  • Dominating, tough or harsh
  • Moody, excitable or unpredictable

In tense meetings, I occasionally:

  • Miss the forest for the trees because I get caught in the details
  • Get swayed by others who may be strong personalities but are not necessarily correct in the situation
  • Attempt to bulldoze my opinion through because I'm frustrated by the process
  • Let my hair down and express feeling s better left unsaid

If I were to speak before a group who didn't know me well, I would hope to leave the impression of:

  • A systematic thinker who could help the group analyse its problems and needs
  • A lively person clearly in touch with the group’s mood and needs, thus able to help make an impact
  • A pragmatic ‘mover’ who could assist the group in solving problems
  • A broad-range thinker capable to making innovative contributions

When I write a letter for business to someone I don't know, I usually try to:

  • Relate my purpose in writing and highlight what I want, need or expect of the other person
  • Analyse the motivations of others and develop a new feel for the situation
  • Relate my purpose in writing and highlight what I want, need or expect of the other person
  • Show my main points and how they work towards our future goals

In any situation, I like to:

  • Keep in mind all the basics or history to date and pinpoint all key obstacles. I modify my game plan after much thinking
  • Analyse the motivations of others and develop a new feel for the situation
  • Review the situation for any deficiencies on my part and take new actions accordingly
  • Create a new hypothesis quickly

In any organisation, I like to:

  • Make my own decisions based on the facts in hand. I can accept direction if I understand the logic behind it.
  • Work collaboratively with people, not alone. I can accept advice and direction from positive authorities
  • Be giving orders or work independently. I don't waste time on, ‘How are we going to do this?' I just want to do it
  • Give my opinions. I can take advice from people already successful in this area, not from people with no, track record

When I am working on a project, I want to:

  • Have time to gather facts and make sure the project develops systematically and logically
  • Be stimulated and involved with people
  • Work with people who want to get results quickly
  • Work with people who are creative and interested in innovation

Sometimes I suspect I may come across to others as being:

  • Overly concerned with specifics to the point of being nit picking
  • Too agreeable or pliable
  • Too dominating or too insensitive
  • Too emotional or too overly dramatic

In communicating with others, I may:

  • Express frustration with those who do not have their facts straight
  • Show little interest with those whose ideas are obviously not thought through and therefore risky
  • Tend to ignore those who talk about 'long range’ and direct my attention to what needs to be done right now
  • Show impatience with ideas that show little originality

I am likely to impress others as:

  • Intellectually orientated and somewhat serious
  • Supportive and dependable
  • Practical and to the point
  • Emotional and somewhat stimulating

When I meet people socially, I am likely to consider whether:

  • They seem thoughtful and reflective
  • They're open and friendly
  • They know what they' re doing and can get things done
  • They're exciting and creative

When faced with people who hold a different point of view, I usually try to:

  • Keep my composure and help others see things logically
  • Place my self in their shoes and see their point of view
  • Rely on my abilities to pull ideas together and convince others of my ideas
  • Find several places we agree on in order to build on these and more towards the future

In terms of how I think about time, I usually concentrate on:

  • Ensuring the actions I take fit into the systematic programme I've set up
  • How what I'm planning may affect other people
  • My immediate actions and whether they work for today
  • My long-range goals or dreams and how to get there. I'm not very disciplined in use of time
