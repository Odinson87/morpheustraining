Behavioural Taster Test

If ever you have wondered why you find it easier to get on with some people, and not others or why members of your team respond better to you it's probably because your styles and approaches are aligned.

What do we mean? Take our Behavioural Taster Test to find out a little more.

In the questions that follow you will be presented with four options. Click one green thumbs up for the option that's most like you, and click one red thumb down, for an option that's least like you.

Start

1. When I meet people socially, I am likely to consider whether:

  • They seem thoughtful and reflective
  • They're open and friendly
  • They know what they' re doing and can get things done
  • They're exciting and creative

2. When faced with people who hold a different point of view, I usually try to:

  • Keep my composure and help others see things logically
  • Place my self in their shoes and see their point of view
  • Rely on my abilities to pull ideas together and convince others of my ideas
  • Find several places we agree on in order to build on these and more towards the future

3. In terms of how I think about time, I usually concentrate on:

  • Ensuring the actions I take fit into the systematic programme I've set up
  • How what I'm planning may affect other people
  • My immediate actions and whether they work for today
  • My long-range goals or dreams and how to get there. I'm not very disciplined in use of time

A 'Thinker'

  • Cautious in actions and decisions
  • Likes organisation and structure
  • Dislikes involvement
  • Asks many questions about specifics and details
  • Prefers objective, task-oriented, intellectual work environment
  • Wants and needs to be right
  • Can be over reliant on data, and facts
  • Works precisely and often alone
  • Good problem solving Skills

A 'Relater'

  • Slower at taking action and making decisions
  • Likes close, personal relationships
  • Dislikes interpersonal conflict
  • Supports and actively listens to others
  • Works slowly and cohesively with others
  • Seeks security and a sense of belonging
  • Communicates trust and confidence
  • Good counselling skills

A 'Director'

  • Decisive in actions and decisions
  • Likes control, dislikes inaction
  • Prefers maximum freedom to manage him/herself and others
  • Cool, independent, and competitive
  • Low tolerance for feelings, attitudes, and advice of others
  • Works quickly and impressively alone
  • Likes challenges and focuses on results
  • Good administrative skills

A 'Socialiser'

  • Spontaneous actions and decisions
  • Likes involvement
  • Dislikes being alone
  • Exaggerates and generalises
  • Tends to dream and likes to share dreams with others
  • Jumps from one activity to another
  • Works quickly and excitedly with others
  • Seeks esteem and acknowledgement
  • Creates excitement and involvement
